## What is a Formal Concept?

In FCA, a **formal concept** is a pair $(A, B)$ where:
1.  $A$ is a subset of objects called the **extent**.
2.  $B$ is a subset of attributes called the **intent**.
3.  The extent $A$ consists of all objects that share *all* attributes in the intent $B$.
4.  The intent $B$ consists of all attributes shared by *all* objects in the extent $A$.

To make this precise, we use derivation operators (denoted by a prime symbol '):
* For a set of objects $X \subseteq G$, its common attributes are $X' = \{m \in M \mid \forall g \in X, (g,m) \in I\}$.
* For a set of attributes $Y \subseteq M$, the objects sharing all these attributes are $Y' = \{g \in G \mid \forall m \in Y, (g,m) \in I\}$.

A pair $(A, B)$ is a formal concept if $A' = B$ and $B' = A$. This means the extent and intent are mutually defining and maximal with respect to each other.
